What is Key Club?

Key Club International is the oldest and largest service program for high school students. This international organization is led by students who learn leadership through their service. Key Club International is sponsored by Kiwanis International, and is currently assisting Kiwanis in helping the children of the world. Right now, there are over 260,000 members from 5,000 clubs from 34 nations.

Why is it called Key Club?

In 1925, the first Key Club was established in Sacramento, California by the local Kiwanis club. Two men from Kiwanis proposed the idea of having a "junior service program," and first opened it to the "key" boys of the school (who wanted to serve the school in any way possible and create more school spirit). Thus, Kiwanis dubbed this organization "Key Club."

What is Kiwanis?

Kiwanis International is an organization dedicated to change the world. They feed the hungry, help shelter the homeless, mentor the disadvantaged, teacher students how to become leaders and help in their community, raise money for research for pediatrics, and make a substantial difference.

About EWHS Key Club

EWHS Key Club is one of the 12 schools that make up DIVISION 21. Division 21 is one of the 44 divisions within the PNW DISTRICT. For more updates on events going on in our division, join the Facebook group "PNW Division 21 Key Club."

When and where are the meetings?

We meet on Tuesdays in the F100 flex at 7:00-8:00 PM!

What kinds of things do we do?

We bake pies for a local church's Thanksgiving dinner, volunteer at events like March of Dimes and the NAMI walk, help out at weekly church dinners, host the "Redeeming Soles" shoe drive, set up an Easter egg hunt in Edmonds, and do fundraisers for our district project (KCCP) and for DCON!

How do I join?

Come to our next meeting (every Tuesday), and be sure to pay your membership due of $18.00 before October 4th.

Why should you join?

You can earn service/CAS hours, make new friends, and go to district conventions filled with other enthusiastic students. Being actively involved with and passionate about Key Club impresses colleges and earns you scholarships. By making a difference in other people's lives, Key Club members build both their character and leadership skills.

March of Dimes improves the health of babies by preventing birth defects, premature birth, and infant mortality. For decades, Key Club members raised thousands of dollars annually through various programs, including collecting change, holding dances and taking part in March of Dimes’ signature event, March for Babies.
