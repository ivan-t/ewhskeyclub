Why should you join?

You can earn service/CAS hours, make new friends, and go to district conventions filled with other enthusiastic students. Being passionate and actively involved about Key Club impresses colleges and earns you scholarships. By making a difference in other people's lives, Key Club members build both their character and leadership skills. Key Club also helps in developing important Social and Emotional Skills, which allows club members to understand self and develop meaningful connections with others. Club members learn to calm down when angry, initiate meaningful friendships, resolve relationship conflicts respectfully, and make ethical and safe choices.

Self-awareness

Accurately accessing one's feelings, interests, values, and strengths/abilities, and maintaining a well-grounded sense of self-confidence. It's especially easy to lose sight of ourselves, but critically important to have it.

Self-management

Regulating one's emotions to handle stress, control impulses, and persevere in overcoming obstacles; setting personal goals and academic goals and then monitoring one's progress toward achieving them. The ability to express emotions constructively.

Relationship skills

Establishing and maintaining healthy and rewarding relationships based on cooperation; resisting inappropriate social pressure; preventing, managing, and resolving interpersonal conflict; and seeking help when needed.

Social awareness

Taking the perspective of empathizing with others; recognizing and appreciating individual and group similarities and differences; identifying the following social standards of conduct; and recognizing and using family, school, and community resources.

Responsible decision-making

Making decisions based on consideration of ethical standards, safety concerns, appropriate standards of conduct, respect for others, and likely consequences of various actions; applying decision-making skills to academic and social situations; and contributing to the well-being of one's school and community.

What kinds of things do we do?

We bake pies for a local church's Thanksgiving dinner, volunteer at events like March of Dimes and the NAMI walk, help out at weekly church dinners, host the "Redeeming Soles" shoe drive, set up an Easter egg hunt in Edmonds, and do fundraisers for our district project (KCCP) and DCON!

Membership Requirements

Come to our next meeting (every Tuesday), pay your membership due ($18.00) before October 4th, and ask one of the officers about Key Club! Remember that in order to be a Key Club member, you must be active! We understand that students have other commitments such as sports and music, however in order to be considered an active member in our Key Club they must meet our requirements. These requirements include:

  • Attending most weekly meetings and stay updated with current service events
  • Have a minimum of 10 service hours per semester
  • 15 hours minimum for those who want to attend DCON
  • Be passionate about Key Club! Caring is our way of life
